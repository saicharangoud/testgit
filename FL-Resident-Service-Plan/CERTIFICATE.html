Influenza is a contagious respiratory illness caused by flu viruses spread mainly by coughing, sneezing and close contact. The illness can be mild to severe, and at times can lead to death. This acute disease comes on suddenly, with self-limiting symptoms (resembling a severe “common cold”) within 2-7 days of onset. Each year thousands of people in the U.S. die from the flu and many more are hospitalized.
